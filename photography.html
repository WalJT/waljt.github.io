My journey into and around photography

I've found it to be a signinficant challenge to write about my photography. Since I treat it as an art form for self expression, meditation, and also a technical area with endless opprotunites for experimentation, my gear and processes are constantly in flux. In the two months or so since I've started writing this I've:

  1. Bought a lens I never thought I'd want
  2. Sold a lens I initialy thought was the bees knees
  3. Went on a trip to one of the most scenic locations in the country, and rushed taking photos to detremental effect
  4. Dove down a huge rabbit hole of youtube videos learning how to process RAW files
  5. Found an old camera and shot a roll of film for the first time in my life
  6. Started experimenting with Film Simulations and more settings in my Fuji camera
  7. Decided processing RAW files is a pain, and that I want to de-couple photography from the computer completely
... So yeah... as you can probably imagine it's qute a challenge to pin down anything concrete i can actually write about.

In addition to my interest in technology, I've always been driven to the outdoors.

  • Studying remotely during Covid with no easily justifiable reason to ever leave the house
  • Somehow ended up watching some youtube videos about landscape photography
  • Bought an entry level digital camera (X-A7 + Kit Lens)
  • Varouis hikes and stuff
  • Photography by walking around and trying out random stuff
  • Street photography in a crowded place is a wild rush
  • Photos tell stories... a picture without people can feel like a stage without actors.
  • Shooting RAW is a pain in the backside... Fuji JPEGS look fantastic
